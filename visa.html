What is a Student Visa?

Student Visas are issued for the duration of the academic course of study or for a period of five years (whichever is less), on the basis of confirmed admission to Universities in India. During this period you will not be permitted to change the purpose of your stay or your institution.
What is a Research Visa?

Research visas are given to Ph.D. students and Research Professor or Scholars and participants attending research conferences/seminars/workshops. Applicants are requested to apply at least 6 weeks before the date of their departure to India and Visas will be valid for entry into India after four weeks of issue. Validity would coincide with the research period. Spouse and dependent family members accompanying the applicant must apply for an Entry Visa (NOT Tourist Visa). The applicant should apply with the subject of their research project, details of places to be visited, previous visits, whether the scholar has secured admission and evidence of financial resources. While applying, the applicant must submit the original letter of admission. Emails or photocopies of admission letters will not be accepted.



It may take up to three months after the submission of the application to get a Visa.
What are the pre-requisites for procuring a Visa?

To apply/be eligible for a Student/Research Visa, please keep in mind the following conditions:



If you are on Government of India scholarships, the respective Indian missions would be advised by Indian Council of Cultural Relations (ICCR) to issue a regular Student/Research Visa after your admission in an Indian university is confirmed.

On the other hand, if you are coming as a self-financing student, you need to produce confirmed letters of admission from university in India for obtaining the regular Student Visa.
Where can I apply for the visa?
One can apply for a visa from any of the Indian Embassies/High Commission. To view the list of Indian Embassies/High Commission. www.mea.gov.in
DurationStudent Visa can be of 5 years or for the duration of the course whichever is earlier.
Rules & regulations that I need to conform to while applying for Student Visa?

Yes, while applying for the visa you need to keep in mind the following regulations:



Passport, valid for a minimum of six months beyond the date of intended departure from India, should accompany your visa application.

Students holding other nationalities (other than the country where applying for visa), should submit proof of long-term (at least three years)/ permanent residence in the country (where applying).

For citizens of other countries, a reference has to be made to their country of residence for which an additional fee is applicable and will involve extra processing time. Please refrain from making inquiries about the status of application during this time.

Student VisaEligibility:

The student must have secured admission to a full-time course at a recognized educational institution in India. This includes universities and colleges in Gautam Buddha Nagar.
The institution where the student is enrolled must be recognized by the Government of India and authorized to admit international students.

Types of Student Visas:

Regular Student Visa (S-1): Issued for full-time degree or diploma courses.
Provisional Student Visa: Issued for students who are yet to confirm their admission in a recognized university.

Key Documents Required:

Offer Letter/Admission Letter: Proof of admission from the Indian university.

Visa Application Form: Filled-out visa application form (available on the Indian government’s visa application website).

Passport: Valid passport with at least six months of validity and two blank pages.

Photographs: Recent passport-sized photographs (check Indian visa specifications).

Proof of Financial Capability: Evidence of funds to support your studies and stay in India, such as bank statements or a financial sponsor’s affidavit.

Health Insurance: Students must provide proof of adequate health insurance that is valid in India.

Educational Documents: Attested copies of academic certificates and transcripts.

Medical Examination: Some applicants may need a medical check-up or certificate, depending on their country of origin.

Processing Time:

Typically, the visa processing time is 3-5 weeks, but it may vary depending on your country and application location.

Registration with FRRO:

After arriving in India, international students must register with the Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) within 14 days of arrival if the visa is for more than 180 days.
How to Apply for a Visa:
Step-by-Step Visa Application Process and Required Documents:

Receive an Offer of Admission:

Apply to a recognized university or college in Gautam Buddha Nagar. Once accepted, obtain an admission letter or offer letter.

Fill Out the Online Visa Application:

Go to the Indian Visa Online Application portal and complete the application form with accurate personal, academic, and travel details. Double-check the information for any mistakes before submitting.

Submit Required Documents:

Upload digital copies of the necessary documents, including:
Admission letter from the university.
Valid passport and photos.
Proof of financial ability.
Travel itinerary (though a return ticket may not be required for long-term students).

Pay the Visa Fee:

The visa processing fee varies by country. Pay the fee as required by your local Indian embassy or visa processing center.

Schedule an Appointment at the Embassy/Consulate:

Once the online application is completed, schedule an in-person interview or document submission appointment at your nearest Indian embassy or consulate.
Bring all original documents along with photocopies for verification.

Attend the Visa Interview:

Prepare for the interview by bringing along:
Passport and visa application confirmation.
Proof of funds, educational certificates, and admission letter.
Health insurance and any other additional documents required by the consulate.
The consulate may ask about your intent to study, financial situation, or ties to your home country.

Wait for Visa Approval:

After the interview and submission, wait for the visa to be processed. You will be notified by the embassy/consulate when it is ready for pickup.

Receive Your Student Visa:

Once approved, collect your passport with the attached student visa from the embassy or consulate.
Work AuthorizationInformation on Working Part-Time as a Student and Post-Study Work Opportunities:

Part-Time Work for International Students:

India does not have a formalized system for part-time work for international students under a student visa, unlike countries such as the UK or Australia.

However, some institutions may offer on-campus internships or work placements, which can be pursued with permission from the university.
Students may also engage in voluntary work or internships, especially if they are related to their field of study. It’s essential to ensure these are non-remunerative unless there is specific approval.

Post-Study Work Opportunities:

After completing their studies, international students in Gautam Buddha Nagar may explore job opportunities. India does not have a post-study work visa category, but students can apply for:

Employment Visa: If they find a job in India, students can convert their student visa into an employment visa, provided they meet the salary threshold and the job role.

Internship Visa: This is for those who want to pursue internships after their studies. The internship must be related to their course and must start within a year of completing their degree.

Entrepreneurship: Graduates with an innovative business idea can apply for an entrepreneur visa to start their own ventures in India.
Extension of Stay:

If you wish to pursue further education in India, you can extend your student visa by providing proof of admission to another academic program.

Frequently asked questions
— Can foreign students come on Tourist Visa to explore admission in some courses/institutes and get Student Visa while in India?
No. In order to enable the foreign students to explore various options to study in India, he/she can apply for provisional Student Visa of 6 months duration from Indian Mission abroad and once he/she gets the confirmed admission while in India, he/she can apply for a Student Visa to the local FRRO/FRO along with necessary documentary evidences of confirmed admission, financial standing etc.
— Can foreign students get a Student Visa directly from Indian Missions abroad?
Yes, they can provided they have confirmed admission to a course in a reputed/recognized institute in India.
— What is the duration of Student Visa?
Student Visa can be of 5 years or for the duration of the course whichever is earlier.
— What are the formalities that foreign students should do on arrival?
Foreign students holding Student Visa of more than 180 days are required to register himself/herself with the local FRRO/FRO within 14 days of arrival.
— Is any approval/NOC required from any other Ministry or Department for technical courses?
So far as admission to diploma/degree/post-graduate degree in engineering and Technology, Architecture & Town Planning, Pharmacy, Applied Arts, MBA, MCA, Hotel Management & Catering Technology are concerned, there is no requirement of NOC from MHRD. However, with respect to courses in medical or para-military courses, NOC from M/o Health will be required
— Is there any restriction on the number of courses that a foreign student can pursue?
No. However, the overall band of the Student Visa regime cannot exceed more than 5 years.
— Can a foreign student change the course or institute or both while in India?
Yes, but with the permission of the local FRRO/FRO and the Institute(s) concerned and subject to no adverse activity.
— Can Student Visa be extended on the ground of clearing supplementaries?
Yes, provided the concerned university/institute permits and suitable documentary evidences to this effect are submitted to the local FRRO/FRO.
— What a foreign student should do for extension of his/her Student Visa?
He/she should approach the local FRRO/FRO with bonafide certificate, proof of adequate financial standing etc.
— How may ‘entries’ are allowed to foreign students?
Normally one entry. However, on bonafide reasons, a foreign student can apply for additional entries at Visa Facilitation Centre, Foreigners Division, Ministry of Home Affairs, Jaisalmer House, 26, Mansingh Road, New Delhi – 110003.
— Can I get the visa even if I don’t have a confirmed admission letter?
You can get a Provisional Student Visa by the Indian missions abroad, on the basis of provisional letters of admissions. However, Provisional Student Visa is valid only for 3 months. Within these 3 months, your admission needs to be confirmed otherwise you will have to leave India.
